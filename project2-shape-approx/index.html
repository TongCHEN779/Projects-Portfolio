Wrapping a shape in a polynomial

Given only a cloud of points, can a single polynomial learn their shape? Yes — build the cloud's moment matrix and its Christoffel–Darboux function stays small on the data and shoots up everywhere else. Its sub-level set wraps the shape, and the higher the polynomial degree, the tighter it hugs — even non-convex shapes with holes. Pick a shape or draw your own.

In Draw mode, click or drag on the canvas to scatter points.

Shape

How it works. From the points we form the empirical moment matrix M = (1/N) Σ v(xᵢ) v(xᵢ)ᵀ, where v(x) stacks all monomials up to the chosen degree. The Christoffel–Darboux polynomial Λ(x) = v(x)ᵀ M⁻¹ v(x) is small where the data lives and large elsewhere; the shaded region is its sub-level set {Λ ≤ τ}, an estimate of the shape. Raise the degree and the boundary curves to fit; raise tightness and it hugs closer. All of this runs live in your browser — no solver needed.

Why I care. This is the data-driven cousin of my research on SOS / level-set shape approximation (Lasserre's moment–SOS machinery), where a polynomial's level set is certified to enclose a semialgebraic set — e.g., bounding the image of a box under a neural network. The certified version solves a semidefinite program; this empirical-moment version gives the same geometric intuition, instantly and interactively.
